# State v. Marcus Webb — Grand Theft Auto

The defendant, Marcus Webb, is charged with stealing a 2024 Tesla Model Y from outside 'The Blue Note' jazz bar on March 14th in New York. A parking-lot camera captured a figure matching Webb's build at 11:47 PM approaching the vehicle. The footage is 480p resolution — the figure's face is not identifiable. Webb claims he was inside the bar until midnight; his alibi is bartender Sarah Lin, who confirms she served him a whiskey sour at 11:30 PM and that his tab was active on a credit card receipt at 11:42 PM. The vehicle owner confirms the Tesla was locked, armed, and parked at 9:00 PM. No fingerprints were recovered from the vehicle. No DNA evidence links Webb to the scene. No stolen property was recovered from Webb's residence.

Key evidence:
- Exhibit A: Parking-lot CCTV footage, 480p, timestamped 11:47 PM, showing a figure matching Webb's height and build
- Exhibit B: Vehicle owner statement confirming the Tesla was locked and parked at 9:00 PM
- Exhibit C: Bar credit card receipt showing Webb's tab active at 11:42 PM
- Officer Daniels' incident report

Witnesses:
- Sarah Lin: Bartender at The Blue Note, alibi witness for the defence. Served Webb at 11:30 PM, confirms tab active at 11:42 PM. Was serving dozens of customers on a busy Friday night and cannot account for Webb's location during every minute.
- Officer Daniels: Responding officer. Arrived at 12:15 AM, reviewed footage on-site. Cannot identify the face in the footage but notes build, height, and gait are consistent with Webb's booking photos.
